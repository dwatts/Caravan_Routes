About the Project

Perhaps one of the most impressive organizational feats of the Poor People’s Campaign was the widespread efforts of demonstrators planning and joining caravans across the nation that travelled to Washington, D.C. These caravans were organized locally and coordinated with the SCLC to map out routes, rally locations, sites to rest, and pick up more demonstrators. Like the entirety of the Poor People’s Campaign, the demonstrators and their activity was watched closely by military informers and journalists. The caravans, much like Resurrection City, were deliberately designed to be highly visible in order to mobilize more participants and raise greater awareness of their causes. The caravan participants were a diverse, multiracial group representing communities across the country. Select a route to see where the caravans stopped and what different causes they might have represented.
